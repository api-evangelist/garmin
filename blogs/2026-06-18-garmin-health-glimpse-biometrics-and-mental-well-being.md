Wearable biometric data helps address mental health challenges affecting over a billion people, with anxiety and depression costing the global economy an estimated $1 trillion annually. The post highlights four applications built on Garmin's health ecosystem: Resilient (an AI-driven employee wellness platform), National Central University of Taiwan's Uedu platform for student stress, A Kind Place (an AI wellness companion for autism support), and Coya (helping first responders manage stress).
